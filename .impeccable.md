## Design Context

### Users
Mixed technical crowd — developers and marketers, skewing technical. People comfortable with APIs, DNS records, and UTM parameters. They use link shorteners as part of their workflow (marketing campaigns, product links, social sharing). Context: working at their desk, comparing options, trying the tool quickly.

### Brand Personality
Bold, precise, unapologetic. slsh.me knows it's a better product than Bitly and doesn't hide it. Confident without being aggressive. Premium tool energy — feels well-made, intentional, and worth trusting with your brand's links.

Three words: **bold, precise, unapologetic**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **References**: Stripe, Vercel — premium, clean, spacious, confident
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS templates, grid backgrounds, gradient text, glassmorphism
- **Theme**: Both light and dark, each designed intentionally
- **Light mode**: Warm off-white (#faf9f7), near-black text, orange accent
- **Dark mode**: Deep warm dark (#0c0c0d), warm off-white text, brighter orange accent
- **Fonts**: Sora (display), Albert Sans (body), JetBrains Mono (code)

### Design Principles
1. **Confidence through typography** — let big, bold type do the talking
2. **Show, don't describe** — product mockups over abstract feature descriptions
3. **Earned simplicity** — clean surfaces backed by real depth
4. **Rhythm through variety** — no two sections should use the same layout
5. **Technical respect** — the audience is technical; don't dumb it down
